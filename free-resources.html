Getting Started

Claude vs ChatGPT: which should I use?

Short answer: you do not need to pick just one. Here is a simple way to decide which to open first.

Use ChatGPT when you want...

  • Quick answers and everyday writing, like emails, captions and short replies.
  • A fast brainstorming partner for ideas, names or promotions.
  • An assistant with strong image generation and mobile app support.

Use Claude when you want...

  • Longer documents drafted or summarised in one go, like reports or grant applications.
  • Careful, well-structured writing for policies, SOPs or board updates.
  • An assistant that tends to reason through detail before answering.

Prompt Library

10 ChatGPT & Claude prompts for small business owners.

Paste these into ChatGPT or Claude, fill in the brackets with your details, and edit the result to sound like you.

  1. Write a service description. "Write a warm, professional 100-word description of [your service] for my website homepage, aimed at [describe your ideal customer]."
  2. Reply to a customer enquiry. "Draft a friendly reply to this customer message: [paste message]. Answer their question, thank them for reaching out, and invite them to book a call."
  3. Draft a social caption. "Write 3 short Instagram captions announcing [your offer], each with a different tone: friendly, confident and playful. Keep each under 40 words."
  4. Summarise a meeting. "Here are my raw meeting notes: [paste notes]. Turn them into a short summary with decisions made and next steps, one line each."
  5. Create a simple SOP. "Write a step-by-step standard operating procedure for [task], so a new team member could follow it without asking questions."
  6. Write a follow-up email. "Draft a polite follow-up email to a client who has not replied in a week about [topic], without sounding pushy."
  7. Draft FAQ answers. "Write clear, friendly answers to these questions my customers often ask: [list questions]."
  8. Write a job ad. "Write a job ad for a [role] at my small business, including day-to-day tasks and a warm closing paragraph inviting people to apply even if they do not tick every box."
  9. Brainstorm a promotion. "Suggest 5 simple promotion ideas for [your business] to run this month, each with almost no budget required."
  10. Proofread website copy. "Proofread this website text for grammar and clarity, and suggest a friendlier alternative if any sentence feels stiff: [paste text]."

Prompt Library

8 AI prompts for non-profit teams.

Written for the everyday writing non-profits do most: reports, updates, thank-yous and program communication.

  1. Draft a grant paragraph. "Write a 150-word paragraph describing the need our program addresses, using the following facts: [paste facts or stats]."
  2. Volunteer thank-you email. "Write a warm thank-you email to volunteers who helped with [event], mentioning the impact of their time without sounding generic."
  3. Board report summary. "Turn these program notes into a short board-ready summary with a highlights section and a risks or needs section: [paste notes]."
  4. Fundraiser social caption. "Write 3 social media captions promoting our fundraiser for [cause], each ending with a clear call to donate or attend."
  5. Event description. "Write a clear, inviting description of our upcoming event [name], including who it is for and what to expect, in under 120 words."
  6. Donor impact update. "Write a short donor update explaining how their support helped with [program or outcome], in a warm, appreciative tone."
  7. Newsletter structure. "Suggest a simple 4-section structure for our monthly newsletter, then draft an opening paragraph using this update: [paste update]."
  8. Program FAQ. "Write plain-language answers to these common questions from families or clients about our program: [list questions]."

Build the Habit

A 5-minute daily AI habit for women in business.

Confidence with AI comes from small, repeated use, not a big course you finish once and forget.

  1. Pick one small task you do every day: a reply, a caption, a note.
  2. Ask ChatGPT or Claude to draft it first, even if the result is not perfect.
  3. Edit rather than write from scratch. It is faster and far less draining.
  4. After a week, notice which tasks you no longer dread. That is the habit sticking.

Five minutes a day, on one task, is enough to build real confidence with AI. No course required, though we run those too.
